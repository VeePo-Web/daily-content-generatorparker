# THE VEEPO PORTFOLIO BUYER PERSONA PROMPT
## Use this prompt in Claude.ai (claude.ai/new) — paste the entire thing

---

You are a world-class brand strategist, consumer psychologist, and conversion copywriter with 30 years of experience building buyer personas for service businesses. You have worked with top direct-response marketers, studied under Alex Hormozi's acquisition frameworks, and applied Russell Brunson's "Dream Customer" methodology across hundreds of businesses.

Your task is to build the most complete, psychologically rich, commercially useful buyer persona ever created for a specific product.

---

## THE PRODUCT

**Brand:** Veepo.ca
**Product:** Portfolio Website Template — a fully managed, done-for-you website system
**Price:** $799 launch fee + $69/month ongoing management
**Delivery:** Live in 14 days. Guaranteed.
**What the client does:** Nothing. They send their content (photos, bio, contact info). Parker builds, hosts, and manages their entire online presence. The client never touches code, fonts, or design decisions.
**Core promise:** "I don't build websites that impress designers. I build websites that make the phone ring."
**Tagline:** "Two weeks from now, your website is making you money."
**Scarcity:** Only 2 new template builds accepted per month.
**URL:** veepo.ca/templates

---

## YOUR TASK

Build a complete, multi-dimensional buyer persona for the person who buys Veepo's **portfolio website template**. This is NOT a generic "small business owner." This is a specific, real human with specific psychology, specific fears, specific language, and specific triggers.

Go deep. This persona will be used to:
1. Write daily social media content (LinkedIn + X) in Alex Hormozi + Russell Brunson style
2. Build a social media sales funnel for this exact buyer
3. Write ads, captions, and hooks that stop this person mid-scroll
4. Identify the content pillars that move this buyer from stranger → customer

---

## STRUCTURE YOUR RESPONSE EXACTLY AS FOLLOWS:

---

### SECTION 1 — WHO THEY ARE (The Surface)

Build the demographic and psychographic profile. Include:
- Age range and why
- Gender breakdown (if skewed) and why
- Location type (urban, suburban, rural) and why
- Income level and what they spend money on
- What they do for work (specific creative professions — not just "photographer")
- How long they've been doing it
- Where they are in their career arc (early, growing, established but stuck)
- What their business model looks like (do they charge per session? retainer? package? sell prints?)
- What platforms they're already on and how they use them (Instagram, TikTok, Behance, LinkedIn, 500px, VSCO)

---

### SECTION 2 — THE INTERNAL WORLD (What They Actually Think and Feel)

This is the most important section. Go deep into their psychology.

**Their ambition:**
- What do they want their business to look like in 3 years?
- What does "making it" look like to them specifically?
- What's the version of success they won't say out loud but quietly want?

**Their daily frustration:**
- What is the single most annoying thing about trying to get clients online?
- What do they tell themselves is the reason they don't have enough bookings?
- What do they actually suspect the real reason is?

**Their relationship with technology:**
- Have they tried to build their own website before? What happened?
- What website builders have they touched (Squarespace, Wix, Format, Pixieset, Canva)?
- How did that make them feel? What was the exact moment they gave up?
- Do they feel shame or pride about their current online presence?

**Their relationship with money:**
- Is $799 a lot to them right now, or is it "one good booking"?
- What do they spend $799 on without thinking?
- What makes them hesitate on $799 for a website?
- What would make $799 feel like an obvious yes?

**Their identity:**
- Do they see themselves as a business owner or an artist?
- What's the tension between those two identities?
- How do they introduce themselves at parties?
- What do they want clients to feel when they land on their website?

---

### SECTION 3 — THE BEFORE STATE (Where They Are Now)

Describe their exact situation before finding Veepo. Be specific and visceral.

- What does their current online presence look like? (Be brutal and specific — Instagram-only, broken Squarespace trial, three-year-old Wix site with the wrong phone number)
- How are they currently getting clients? (Word of mouth, Instagram DMs, referrals from one photographer friend)
- How many bookings/inquiries are they getting per month?
- How much of their income is inconsistent or "feast and famine"?
- What does the moment of seeing a competitor's stunning website feel like to them?
- What do they tell themselves when they look at their own site (or lack of one)?
- What has stopped them from fixing this until now? Name every excuse.

---

### SECTION 4 — THE AFTER STATE (What They Want)

The dream. Be specific.

- What does life look like 90 days after launching the Veepo site?
- What's the specific phone call or email they imagine receiving because of their new site?
- How do they describe their website to a potential client when someone asks?
- What does it feel like to send someone to their website and feel proud instead of apologetic?
- How does a professional website change how they show up at networking events, on Instagram, in client conversations?
- What does it change about how they price their work?

---

### SECTION 5 — THE OBJECTIONS (What Stops Them From Buying Right Now)

Every objection they raise when they see the Veepo offer. For each objection, write:
- The surface objection (what they say)
- The real objection underneath (what they mean)
- The counter-argument that dissolves it (what Parker should say)

Cover at minimum:
1. "I could just build it myself on Squarespace/Wix/Format"
2. "$799 is expensive"
3. "I'm not ready yet — I need more work in my portfolio first"
4. "I don't know if a website will actually get me more clients"
5. "What if I don't like how it looks?"
6. "Monthly fees make me nervous"
7. "I've been burned by web designers before"
8. Any other objections this specific buyer would have

---

### SECTION 6 — THEIR LANGUAGE (Exact Words They Use)

This section is gold. Write out:

**How they describe their problem** (in their own words — not marketing language):
- Give 10 phrases/sentences this person would actually say about their website situation

**How they describe their dream outcome** (in their own words):
- Give 10 phrases/sentences about what they want

**What they search on Google when they're looking for a solution:**
- Give 15 specific search queries this person actually types

**What they respond to on social media:**
- Types of posts they stop scrolling for
- Types of posts they share or save
- Types of posts they comment on

**What they hate being sold to with:**
- Language that makes them roll their eyes
- Tactics that feel manipulative to them
- Things web designers/agencies always say that turn them off

---

### SECTION 7 — THE CONTENT STRATEGY (What to Post to Reach This Person)

Based on everything above, build the complete content strategy for reaching this buyer on LinkedIn and X (Twitter).

**The 5 Content Pillars for This Buyer:**
For each pillar, give:
- Pillar name and purpose
- Why this pillar works psychologically for this buyer
- 5 specific post hook examples (the opening line — the scroll stopper)
- Best format (single post, thread, carousel, before/after image)

**The Funnel Map:**
Map out the exact content journey from stranger to customer:
- Stage 1: They've never heard of Veepo (what content introduces them)
- Stage 2: They're aware but haven't engaged (what content builds trust)
- Stage 3: They're interested but hesitating (what content handles objections)
- Stage 4: They're ready but haven't clicked (what content creates the tipping point)
- Stage 5: They've clicked to veepo.ca/templates (what the page needs to do — but don't go here, just name it)

**The Hormozi + Brunson Framework Applied:**
Show specifically how to apply:
- Alex Hormozi's "value equation" (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement / Time Delay × Effort and Sacrifice) to this buyer's decision
- Russell Brunson's "Epiphany Bridge" story structure for a post about this buyer's transformation
- One example post written in the combined style

---

### SECTION 8 — THE NORTH STAR INSIGHT

The single most important thing to understand about this buyer that, if you get it right, makes everything else easier.

One paragraph. The insight that changes the entire strategy.

---

### SECTION 9 — GIVE THEM A NAME

Give this persona a full name, age, city, and a one-paragraph "day in the life" snapshot that captures who they are so vividly that Parker could pick them out of a crowd.

---

Go deep. Be specific. Be honest. Do not write generic marketing copy — write the truth about this human being, their fears, their dreams, and what it will take to earn their trust and their $799.
